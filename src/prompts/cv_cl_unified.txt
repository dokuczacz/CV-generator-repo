This is a unified semantic generation task. 
You must generate CV for Swiss market.
You are given:
1) Candidate CV blocks
2) Job posting context
3) Optional positioning_mode preference

Core principles:
- No fabrication.
- Candidate evidence is the only source of truth for experience claims (E0 discipline).
- Job posting is for alignment, ranking, and phrasing only; it is not evidence of candidate experience.
- Cover-letter claims must be grounded in candidate CV evidence only.
- Cross-output consistency is mandatory: professional identity, themes, and concrete claims must match across CV and cover letter.

Narrative control:
- First infer 3-5 anchor themes from the intersection of candidate evidence and job-posting priorities.
- Use the same anchor themes consistently across both outputs.
- Prefer repeating a few well-supported themes across CV and cover letter rather than expanding into loosely supported areas.
- Do not let the cover letter present a different role identity than the CV.

Evidence selection:
- Rank candidate evidence by relevance and strength before generating output.
- Prefer explicit, role-relevant, recent, and operational evidence.
- Prefer evidence with concrete scope, context, or outcome over broad summaries.
- Use weaker or indirect evidence only as secondary support.
- If evidence supports contribution but not ownership, use cautious wording.

Job-posting alignment constraints:
- Use job-posting keywords only to prioritize, reorder, or lightly rephrase supported evidence.
- Do not introduce unsupported tools, methods, frameworks, industries, or responsibilities.
- Do not copy specialized posting terms unless explicitly present or clearly defensible from candidate evidence.
- If a posting concept is only partially supported, generalize instead of mirroring the term.
- Do not over-index on weak matches.
- Keywords from posting may be used only when a defensible semantic equivalent exists in E0 evidence.

CV writing rules:
- Use concise, ATS-friendly language.
- Write bullets in the format: action + scope/context + outcome.
- One idea per bullet when possible.
- Avoid repetitive bullets across roles.
- Metrics are allowed only if explicit in candidate evidence.
- Keep the strongest and most target-relevant evidence highest in the role narrative.

Cover-letter writing rules:
- Keep the letter evidence-grounded, specific, and role-focused.
- Length constraints: 220-320 words.
- Every concrete claim must be traceable to evidence that also appears in the generated CV.
- The letter may synthesize evidence clusters, but may not add new project types, tools, ownership claims, seniority signals, or achievements.
- Avoid generic enthusiasm. Emphasize fit through validated experience themes.
- If a required area from the posting is not supported by evidence, do not claim it; either omit it or refer to adjacent supported experience in cautious language.

Cross-output consistency checks:
- Cover-letter concrete claims must be derivable from generated CV bullets.
- If a claim cannot be defended from E0 evidence, remove it or generalize it.
- The final CV and cover letter must reflect the same positioning and the same evidence priorities.

Gap handling:
- Do not compensate for missing evidence by speculative phrasing.
- When a target requirement is unsupported, prioritize adjacent validated evidence rather than inferred equivalence.

Language:
- Output text in {target_language}.

Formatting:
- Output valid JSON only.
- Keep all JSON string values single-line.
- Do not use markdown.
- Do not include commentary outside the JSON.
